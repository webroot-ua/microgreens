What is Microgreens

Microgreens are young plants that are somewhere between sprouts and young leafy vegetables. It takes only 2-3 weeks from sowing to harvest. It has a rich aromatic taste and a concentrated content of nutrients in a bioavailable form, which is several times higher than the level of trace elements and vitamins in mature plants and fruits.
This makes it a good addition to any diet and the basis of a healthy eating culture. We are what we consume!

Scientific facts about Microgreens:

  • Microgreens contain a large amount of important vitamins (including vitamins A, C, and K) and minerals such as calcium, iron, and magnesium.
  • High levels of antioxidants: This helps protect your body from free radicals that can damage cells.
  • Support for the immune system: Microgreens contain components that boost your immune system and make you less vulnerable to disease.
  • Improved digestion: The fiber content of microgreens promotes healthy digestion and bowel function.
  • Reducing inflammation: The active ingredients in microgreens can help reduce inflammation in the body.
  • Improving heart health: Microgreens contain compounds that may help lower cholesterol and improve heart health.
  • Increased energy: The high concentration of vitamins and minerals can help boost energy and stamina.
  • Support for healthy skin: Antioxidants in microgreens help maintain a youthful appearance and reduce the appearance of wrinkles.
  • Reducing the risk of disease: Regular consumption of microgreens helps reduce the risk of various diseases, including diabetes and cancer.
  • Improved vision: Vitamin A in microgreens promotes healthy vision and prevents growth problems.
  • Maintaining a healthy weight: Microgreens can help control appetite and help maintain a healthy weight.
  • A balanced diet: Adding microgreens to your diet enriches it with nutrients that promote overall health and well-being.

WHEATGRASS

Young green wheat shoots 2-3 weeks old are the raw material for making healthy food products, usually in the form of juice or powder from dried sprouts. Wheat sprout juice contains a number of vitamins, micro- and macronutrients, and minerals necessary for humans, enzymes, and amphino acids.
It is perfectly absorbed by the body, best of all in the form of freshly cut shoots or juice - fresh or frozen.

Wheatgrass loses some of its beneficial properties when dehydrated!
